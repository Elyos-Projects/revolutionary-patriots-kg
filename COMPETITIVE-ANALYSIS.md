# Competitive & Improvement Analysis — `revolutionary-patriots-kg`

> Scope confirmed from `planning/projects/revolutionary-patriots-kg/PLAN.md` (v0.2.0, 2026-06-28) and `TASKS.md` (v0.2.0). The project is an **open, linked, citable RDF/JSON-LD knowledge graph of American Revolution patriots** — people, military service, units, engagements, places, kinship, and the documentary sources that prove each claim — built **only** from public-domain / openly-licensed primary sources, with **provenance on every assertion**, conflict-faithful representation, Wikidata reconciliation, and a hard licensing/ToS gate that puts the proprietary **DAR GRS** and **SAR PRS** databases categorically out of scope. Donated lane; risk tier **medium** (license/ToS primary gate + historical-accuracy/citation review secondary gate). Note the project's framing is **genealogical/military-service**, not a general NPOV social-history graph — that framing choice is itself a central finding below.

---

## 1. Correctness & completeness review of PLAN.md

The plan is unusually rigorous on the dimensions Elyos cares most about — licensing, provenance, and "delivered ≠ merged." Findings, grouped:

**Strong / correct as written**
- **Provenance-per-assertion as a hard CI gate** (100% target; un-sourced assertions never published) with the countable "assertion unit" explicitly tied to the M0 provenance-mechanism decision (named-graphs+PROV-O vs RDF-star). This is the single best-engineered part of the plan: it makes "provenance on every assertion" mechanically checkable rather than aspirational. Correct and rare.
- **Licensing gate** (`sources/allowlist.yml`, per-copy/edition rights analysis, named License/ToS reviewer as hard M0 exit, anti-laundering controls: page/image-level citations, contributor attestation, GRS/PRS-distinctive-field spot-checks). The "PD original ≠ PD scan" caveat and the explicit Fold3/Ancestry-terms exclusion (M1 entry precondition: NARA-served digitizations only) are exactly right and are the most common way open-history projects accidentally breach licenses.
- **Conflict-faithful modeling** (every contested fact retains all sourced variants; no single-winner without sourced editorial rationale) and **precision-over-recall dedup** (explicit blocking keys, zero-confirmed-false-merge target, reversible merges). Both correct; the false-merge-corrupts-the-graph reasoning is sound.
- **No-original-research boundary** is stated as a non-goal ("structures what sources attest"; "AI-generated 'fill-in' of unknown facts" explicitly out of scope; "gaps stay gaps"). Good — this is the correct posture for a sourcing-driven KG.

**Gaps / weaknesses (most important first)**

1. **NPOV / anti-mythologizing / inclusion is underspecified — the biggest substantive gap.** The plan handles *factual* conflict faithfully but is largely silent on *framing* and *interpretive* NPOV, which is where Revolutionary-era history is genuinely charged. Specific holes:
   - The very term **"patriot"** is a point-of-view label. The plan adopts it uncritically as the central class name and selection criterion. Whether someone was a "patriot," a "rebel," or a loyal subject is contested and perspective-dependent. The graph should model **allegiance/role as a sourced, qualified property** (Patriot / Loyalist / neutral / contested / changed-over-time), not bake one side's label into the ontology and the scope filter.
   - **Inclusion of enslaved people, women, Indigenous nations, and Loyalists is absent from the data model and scope.** The seven core classes (Person, Service, MilitaryUnit, Engagement, Place, Document, KinshipRelation) and the seed sources (NARA pension files, lineage books) structurally encode the demographic that *received federal pensions and DAR/SAR recognition* — overwhelmingly white men who served the Patriot side. A graph seeded this way will silently reproduce that selection bias as if it were the historical record. There is no class or property for enslaved status, no plan to ingest Loyalist or Black-Loyalist sources, and no NPOV/inclusion review gate. This is both a historical-accuracy problem (the graph misrepresents who was present at the founding) and an Elyos-guardrail problem (the CLAUDE.md refusal rules name discrimination and presentist/exclusionary distortion).
   - **No "anti-mythologizing" or presentism review** exists alongside the citation review. The accuracy reviewer checks *whether a claim matches its source*; nobody checks *whether the selection, labeling, and framing nationalize or hagiographize*. Recommend a named **NPOV/contested-framing reviewer (historian)** dimension, distinct from citation review, with authority over class names, allegiance labeling, inclusion coverage, and any narrative/derived fields.
2. **Ontology choice (schema.org + Wikidata) is pragmatic but under-powered for events and provenance vs. the field standard.** The cultural-heritage and historical-KG world has largely standardized on **CIDOC-CRM** (event-centric: people/units participate in spatio-temporally bounded events) — which is a near-perfect fit for "service episodes" and "engagements," and is what the closest analog (WarSampo) uses. The plan should at minimum evaluate CIDOC-CRM (or its `crm` event pattern) and **PROV-O** alignment explicitly in the M0 ontology task, and document why schema.org-only was chosen if it is. As written, the ontology task risks shipping a bespoke vocabulary that's harder for libraries/archives (the intended stewards) to consume than a CIDOC-CRM-aligned one.
3. **"Verified need" is honestly flagged as `false` — but that makes the flagship status risky.** The plan is admirably candid that the *gap* is real but no committed steward/beneficiary exists, and gates "shipped" on securing one. Correct posture, but for a *flagship* repo this is a real delivery risk: 5,000 sourced nodes with no adopting library/historical-society is "maintained-but-not-shipped." Treat steward outreach as M0 critical-path, not parallel.
4. **Beneficiary framing leans genealogical, which collides with the NPOV mission.** Genealogists and DAR/SAR-adjacent users want *lineage proof*; an NPOV social-historical commons wants *coverage and balance*. These pull in different directions (e.g., genealogists care about pension-eligible ancestors; historians care about the enslaved man who appears only as property in that same pension file). The plan should state which master it serves when they conflict — and the guardrails imply the historical-commons master should win.
5. **Citation-audit frame is good but accuracy ≠ representativeness.** The ≥95%-verify, stratified, independent-auditor frame audits *whether shipped assertions match their sources*. It does not audit *whether the graph's coverage is representative or whether framing is neutral*. Add a coverage/representativeness audit (e.g., share of persons who are non-Patriot, non-white, non-male where sources exist) to the metrics table.
6. **Minor / mechanical:** (a) CC0-vs-CC-BY-SA segregation at the *statement* level is asserted "where feasible" — feasibility should be decided in M0, since a single CC-BY-SA-derived label can contaminate a CC0 dataset's license claim; (b) the persistent-IRI (w3id/PURL) decision is good and resolves the orphaning risk; (c) reviewer-burnout throttle (throughput ceiling) is a genuinely good operational control and should be kept.

## 2. Competitive landscape (researched, with sources)

No existing project is *exactly* "an open, provenance-per-assertion linked graph of Revolutionary patriots." But the plan's executive summary slightly overstates the white space — several projects occupy large adjacent territory, and two are direct methodological templates.

**General-purpose open KGs**
- **Wikidata** — CC0 collaborative KG; the reconciliation target the plan correctly leans on. Strength: enormous, free, reconcilable, multilingual labels, QIDs as global identifiers. Weakness for this domain: **provenance/reference quality is uneven** — a well-known study found ~61% of evaluated references were relevant and authoritative, i.e. roughly **40% were not**, and many statements lack references at all ([Piscopo/Kaffee et al., ISWC 2017](https://eprints.soton.ac.uk/412923/1/WD_sources_iswc_7_.pdf)). Historical-biography modeling in Wikidata is contested and bias-prone — see the aptly-titled ["'I have always found the whole area a minefield': Wikidata, historical lives, and knowledge infrastructure"](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s42803-024-00090-5) (PMC mirror: [PMC12084240](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12084240/)), which documents how date/identity/category modeling forces presentist or single-truth choices on inherently contested lives. **This is the plan's core differentiator: enforced page/image-level provenance on *every* assertion and conflict-faithful representation — exactly what Wikidata does not guarantee.**
- **DBpedia** — extracts structured data from Wikipedia infoboxes (~400M+ facts from English Wikipedia). Strength: scale, RDF-native, linked. Weakness: it inherits Wikipedia's secondary-source framing and infobox errors, has **no per-assertion primary-source provenance, no temporal model, and known extraction-accuracy limits**; star-shaped query limits ([DBpedia overview/limitations](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1812.10315)). Not a primary-source graph.

**Authoritative document corpora (PD, the plan's intended fuel)**
- **Founders Online (NARA / NHPRC)** — 180,000+ PD documents (Washington, Franklin, Adams, Jefferson, Hamilton, Madison correspondence), downloadable as JSON with a data dictionary ([Founder's Online metadata, archives.gov](https://www.archives.gov/open/nhprc/dataset-founders-online); [data.gov mirror](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/founders-online-metadata)). Strength: authoritative, PD, structured-ish, person/document links. Weakness: it's a *document corpus*, not an entity graph — no Service/Unit/Engagement model, elite-correspondent-centric. **An ideal early integration, already in the backlog (`data-003`).**
- **NARA Catalog** — Revolutionary War pension/bounty-land (M804) and compiled service records. PD federal records, but the full digitizations are indexed and served behind **Fold3/Ancestry** subscriptions (free only inside NARA facilities or at FamilySearch) ([Fold3 has 100% of M804](https://blog.fold3.com/revolutionary-war-pension-files/); [archives.gov genealogy/partners](https://www.archives.gov/research/genealogy/start-research/nara-resources)). **The plan's M1 hard precondition — NARA-served PD-clear scans, not Fold3/Ancestry copies — is precisely the correct guardrail and confirms the competitive risk is real.**

**Direct methodological templates (the strongest comparables)**
- **WarSampo** — Finnish WWII as Linked Open Data: ~14M triples, public SPARQL endpoint, in the LOD Cloud, **CIDOC-CRM-extended event ontology** modeling war as spatio-temporal events that soldiers/units participate in, with a repeatable rebuild pipeline ([WarSampo, Semantic Web Journal 12(2), 2021](https://www.semantic-web-journal.net/content/warsampo-knowledge-graph-finland-second-world-war-linked-open-data-0)). **This is the closest existing analog to what the plan wants to build, in a different war.** Strength: proves the model end-to-end; great architecture template. Weakness/opportunity: not US-focused, not provenance-per-assertion in the strict sense the plan demands. The plan should study WarSampo as a reference architecture (and likely adopt its CIDOC-CRM event pattern).
- **Mapping the Republic of Letters (Stanford CESTA)** — ~80,000-entity graph of Enlightenment correspondence/people/places, moved relational→graph, spawned the **Palladio** visualization suite ([republicofletters.stanford.edu](http://republicofletters.stanford.edu/); [DH Specialist project page](https://dhs.stanford.edu/project-gallery/mapping-the-republic-of-letters/)). Strength: humanities-grade modeling of people/places/correspondence in exactly this era; visualization tooling the plan could reuse. Weakness: project/period-specific, not a maintained open API, lighter on per-assertion provenance.
- **Six Degrees of Francis Bacon** — ~13,000 people / ~200,000 inferred relationships in early-modern Britain, extracted from the ODNB via NLP + statistical inference, open dataset (Folger / Kaggle) ([DHQ paper](https://www.digitalhumanities.org/dhq/vol/10/3/000244/000244.html); [Folger dataset](https://collections.folger.edu/detail/Six-Degrees-of-Francis-Bacon-Dataset/73765a5b-ab9c-4939-8ae7-f0ef7e9ebf6e)). Strength: prosopography-at-scale precedent; open data. **Weakness is instructive**: its relationships are *statistically inferred*, not source-attested — the opposite of this plan's no-original-research/provenance posture. A useful "what we explicitly will not do" foil.

**Cultural-heritage KG infrastructure (the standards/tooling layer)**
- **CIDOC-CRM + ResearchSpace + American Art Collaborative / Prado / Europeana (EDM)** — CIDOC-CRM is the ISO-standard event-centric ontology for museums/archives/libraries; ResearchSpace (British Museum, open source) is a CIDOC-CRM-native collaborative semantic platform; the American Art Collaborative published 14 US museums' holdings as CIDOC-CRM LOD ([Ontotext case study](https://www.ontotext.com/knowledgehub/case-studies/prestigeous-cultural-heritage-organizations-imporved-collaborative-research/); [Aalto Wikibase/CIDOC paper](https://seco.cs.aalto.fi/publications/2022/kesaniemi-et-al-wikibase-2022.pdf)). **Relevance: these are what the plan's intended stewards (libraries, archives, DH centers) already speak.** Aligning to CIDOC-CRM is the difference between "a steward can ingest us" and "a steward has to write a mapping."

**Inclusive / under-represented-history databases (the plan's biggest blind spot, and biggest opportunity)**
- **Enslaved.org: Peoples of the Historical Slave Trade (Michigan State / Matrix, Mellon-funded)** — a **Wikibase-based Linked Open Data hub** of **600,000+ people records and ~5M data points** centering named enslaved and freed individuals, explicitly serving genealogists/scholars/public ([enslaved.org/about](https://enslaved.org/about); [Wikimedia DE write-up](https://www.wikimedia.de/2020/en/themen/wikibase-enslaved/)). **This is simultaneously the strongest "gap we should fill" partner and a model for inclusive LOD.** It proves the technical and ethical pattern the plan is missing.
- **Black Loyalist / "Book of Negroes" databases** — the 1783 inspection roll of ~3,000 self-emancipated Black Loyalists evacuated by the British; searchable databases at **Library and Archives Canada** (~2,831 names), the **Black Loyalist Digital Collection**, and a transcribed **Black Loyalist Directory, 1783–1788** at American Ancestors ([LAC Book of Negroes](https://www.bac-lac.gc.ca/eng/discover/military-heritage/loyalists/book-of-negroes/Pages/introduction.aspx); [TNA UK](https://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/explore-the-collection/stories/the-book-of-negroes/); [American Ancestors DB](https://dbnews.americanancestors.org/2023/08/03/new-database-black-loyalist-directory-1783-1788/)). **Largely PD-eligible primary sources that the current plan's "patriot/pension" framing would entirely exclude** — yet these are the documentary record for Black participants in the Revolution.
- **DAR GRS / SAR PRS** — the proprietary incumbents the plan correctly walls off. Confirmed: GRS is a free-to-search-but-access-controlled compiled database (richer features only inside the DAR Library), and SAR PRS explicitly states its compiled entries are **not acceptable as proof** and must be traced back to original sources ([DAR GRS](https://www.dar.org/research/genealogy-resources/using-genealogical-research-system-grs); [SAR PRS](https://www.sar.org/patriot-research-system/)). This *validates* the plan's strategy on two fronts: (a) scraping them is a ToS/compiled-DB-copyright hazard, and (b) **even SAR tells users the underlying PD sources are the real authority — which is exactly the open layer this project would build.**

## 3. Gaps we can fill

1. **The missing open, provenance-first patriot/service graph itself.** No open KG links a person → unit → engagement → pension file → documented kin with a resolvable PD citation on each edge. Real gap.
2. **An *inclusive* Revolution graph.** No open graph integrates Patriots, Loyalists, Black Loyalists, enslaved people named in pension/property records, women, and Indigenous nations under one sourced, NPOV model. Enslaved.org and the Black Loyalist DBs cover slices; nobody links them to the military-service spine. **This is the highest-value, most differentiated, and most guardrail-aligned gap.**
3. **Provenance-per-assertion as a product feature.** Wikidata/DBpedia can't promise it; the plan's CI gate can. Make it visible (every node in the explorer shows its source image/page).
4. **A reconciliation bridge.** A clean, QID-reconciled US-Revolution layer back into Wikidata is a contribution Wikidata itself benefits from (the plan's "downstream open projects" beneficiary).
5. **A reusable, steward-ingestible vocabulary.** A CIDOC-CRM/PROV-O-aligned, well-documented ontology + SHACL shapes is itself reusable infrastructure for *any* military/biographical-service KG (see §7).

## 4. Differentiators to win (vs Wikidata specifically)

1. **Enforced provenance on 100% of assertions, at page/image granularity** — a hard CI gate, not a best-effort `reference` like Wikidata's (~40% of which fail relevance/authority). This is the single strongest differentiator.
2. **Conflict-faithful by design** — contested facts keep all sourced variants; Wikidata's preferred/normal/deprecated ranking pushes toward a single "best" value and struggles with genuinely contested lives ("minefield" paper).
3. **Curated, license-gated source provenance** — every datum traces to a PD source whose rights were individually analyzed; Wikidata mixes sourced and unsourced freely.
4. **NPOV-by-construction inclusion (if §1 fix is adopted)** — allegiance as a sourced qualified property + deliberate inclusion of enslaved/Loyalist/women/Indigenous perspectives, reviewed by a historian. Wikidata's coverage is whatever editors happen to add.
5. **Domain-tuned ontology** (Service/Unit/Engagement event model) vs Wikidata's generic properties — easier to query "everyone in the 2nd Maryland at Camden" with full citations.
6. **Steward-grade exports** (RDF/Turtle + JSON-LD under a persistent w3id/PURL namespace, SHACL-validated) aimed at libraries/archives, not just the wiki community.

Caveat to internalize: **don't try to out-scale Wikidata.** Win on trust, provenance, NPOV, and domain depth — explicitly reconcile *to* Wikidata rather than competing for breadth.

## 5. Claude API leverage (and hard boundaries)

The donated lane forbids the CLI from running an agent headless, so Claude here is an **assistive contributor tool** (the human runs their own agent) and/or a **funded-lane metered helper** for bounded extraction batches — never an autonomous authority over historical fact. (Reference model for any code the project ships: `claude-opus-4-8` with adaptive thinking, `output_config.format` for structured extraction, and `citations` content blocks when reading PD documents — see the project's Claude API skill.)

**Where Claude genuinely helps (always human-/historian-reviewed):**
1. **Provenance-bound entity/relation extraction from PD sources.** Use Claude with **document `citations: {enabled: true}`** so every extracted Person/Service/Unit/Engagement field comes back with the exact `cited_text` + page/char location from the source image/transcript — feeding the page/image-level provenance the CI gate demands. Use **strict structured outputs** (`output_config.format` / a JSON schema per ontology class) so extraction yields schema-valid records. Critically, instruct Claude to **flag-on-doubt and never guess** (illegible field → `null` + flag), matching the plan's "gaps stay gaps." This is the highest-value, best-fit use.
2. **Reconciliation candidate generation to Wikidata.** Claude proposes QID matches with rationale and confidence (normalized name + service state/unit + period as blocking features), feeding the human-confirmed merge worklist. Precision-tuned; Claude proposes, human disposes.
3. **NPOV / framing / mythologizing checks (advisory).** Claude as a *first-pass linter* over labels and any derived/editorial text: flag POV terms ("hero," "traitor," uncritical "patriot"), presentist framing, missing-perspective coverage (e.g., "this pension file names an enslaved person — create a sourced Person?"), and CC-BY-SA contamination of CC0 fields. Output is a flag queue for the historian reviewer, never an auto-edit.
4. **Anti-laundering assist.** Claude can help spot GRS/PRS-distinctive compiled artifacts (distinctive normalizations/derived fields) for reviewer spot-checks — surfacing candidates, not adjudicating.
5. **OCR/transcription QA and citation-faithfulness pre-screen.** Claude diffs a transcription against the source image and flags low-confidence characters/fields before the human citation audit (it raises suspects; it doesn't pass the batch).

**Where Claude must NOT decide (hard boundaries):**
- **Historical facts** — never asserted on Claude's own knowledge; every fact must trace to a reviewed reliable PD source. No model-"known" dates, kinships, or service records.
- **No original historical research or gap-filling** — Claude may not infer, interpolate, or "complete" unknown facts (the Six Degrees of Francis Bacon inference approach is explicitly out of scope here).
- **NPOV / contested-framing calls** — final allegiance labeling, inclusion/exclusion decisions, and any narrative framing are decided by a **historian reviewer**, not the model.
- **Citation faithfulness sign-off** — the ≥95% stratified audit is performed by an **independent human auditor**; Claude pre-screens but cannot grade its own (or anyone's) extractions.
- **License/PD determinations** — the named License/ToS reviewer approves every allow-list entry; Claude may summarize a rights situation but never marks a source `approved`.
- **Merges** — no auto-merge ships; zero-confirmed-false-merge requires human confirmation.
- **No fabricated history** — refuse/flag any task that would invent, embellish, or nationalize the record; never write secrets/keys into receipts or logs (Elyos rule).

## 6. Ten concrete optimizations

1. **Reframe "Patriot" as a sourced, qualified `Allegiance/Role` property** (Patriot / Loyalist / neutral / contested / changed-over-time) instead of a baked-in class name and scope filter. Rename the project's *internal* selection criterion accordingly even if the repo name stays. (Fixes the §1.1 NPOV finding.)
2. **Add an `EnslavedStatus` / `civil-condition` sourced property and a deliberate inclusion mandate** for enslaved people, women, Indigenous individuals/nations, and Loyalists — with a coverage metric in the success table. Seed it by integrating Black Loyalist / Enslaved.org-style sources, not just pension files.
3. **Evaluate and most likely adopt CIDOC-CRM event modeling** (people/units *participate in* spatio-temporal Service and Engagement events) + **PROV-O** for provenance, aligning to the standard the stewards and WarSampo already use; document the decision in the M0 ontology task.
4. **Add a named NPOV/contested-framing reviewer role** (historian), distinct from the citation reviewer, with veto over class names, allegiance labels, inclusion coverage, and any derived/narrative fields. Add an anti-mythologizing/presentism checklist to the review gate.
5. **Add a representativeness/coverage audit** alongside the ≥95% citation audit (e.g., % of persons who are Loyalist / non-white / women where sources exist), reported quarterly — so the graph's *shape* is audited, not just its *accuracy*.
6. **Integrate Founders Online early as the proof-of-pipeline second source** (PD, JSON-with-data-dictionary, person↔document links) — lower-risk than handwritten pension OCR and already in the backlog; promote `data-003` toward M1/M2.
7. **Study WarSampo as the reference architecture** for the rebuild-from-source pipeline and event ontology; reuse **Palladio** (from Mapping the Republic of Letters) for the explorer to avoid building visualization from scratch.
8. **Decide CC0-vs-CC-BY-SA segregation at the statement level in M0, not "where feasible."** Default CC0; route any CC-BY-SA-derived statement into a clearly-labeled partition so the CC0 claim is never contaminated. Prefer Wikidata (CC0) over Wikipedia text for imported statements.
9. **Make provenance a visible product feature in the explorer**: every assertion renders its source thumbnail/page-image and license. This *is* the differentiator vs Wikidata — show it.
10. **Pursue Enslaved.org, a Black-Loyalist project, or a university DH center as the committed steward** in M0 critical-path. These partners are mission-aligned, already do inclusive LOD, and would adopt/cite — directly closing the "verified need / no steward" risk that blocks "shipped."

## 7. Parallel & perpendicular spin-offs

**Parallel (same method, adjacent corpus):**
- **`historical-markers-index`** — engagements/places in this graph reconcile naturally to physical historical markers; a shared Place gazetteer (aligned to Wikidata) serves both. The patriot graph's Engagement/Place layer can seed or consume marker data.
- **`local-history-stubs`** — county/town-level PD records (town histories, muster rolls) are a natural source-batch extension; the same allow-list/provenance/extraction pipeline applies unchanged.
- **`genealogy-public-records`** — kinship + vital-records modeling is shared; the KinshipRelation class and conflict-faithful kinship pattern are directly reusable, and this is the natural home for the genealogy beneficiary class without contaminating the NPOV history graph.
- **`loc-public-domain-engine`** — Library of Congress PD manuscript/print ingestion is already an approved source class; a shared LoC intake/OCR-QA component (with Claude-assisted, citation-bound extraction) serves multiple projects.

**Perpendicular (reusable infrastructure spun out of this project):**
- **An "Open History KG" template/starter** — the ontology (CIDOC-CRM/PROV-O-aligned), SHACL shapes, `allowlist.yml` schema, provenance-completeness CI linter, and persistent-IRI (w3id/PURL) pattern, packaged so *any* sourced historical KG (a different war, region, or period — cf. WarSampo) can start with the guardrails pre-built. This is arguably the most valuable durable asset.
- **An MCP server** exposing the graph as queryable tools (`find_person`, `service_for_person`, `sources_for_assertion`, `reconcile_to_wikidata`) so other agents/Claude can consume the sourced, NPOV graph with provenance attached to every result — turning the dataset into reusable infrastructure for the wider open ecosystem.
- **A reusable provenance-bound extraction harness** (Claude + document citations + strict structured outputs + flag-on-doubt) usable by every Elyos open-data project that turns PD documents into structured, cited records.

## 8. Open questions

1. **Naming & framing:** does the project keep the POV-laden "patriots" name, or reframe to an inclusive "American Revolution people/service" graph with allegiance as a sourced property? (Recommend the latter; resolve before the ontology ships.)
2. **Inclusion scope:** are Loyalists, Black Loyalists, enslaved people, women, and Indigenous nations *in scope from M0*, or a later phase? (The guardrails argue for from-the-start; the current sources argue for later — this tension must be decided.)
3. **Ontology standard:** schema.org-only, or CIDOC-CRM + PROV-O alignment? (Affects steward ingestibility and the countable-assertion unit.)
4. **Steward identity:** is the committed adopter a genealogical body, a library/archive, a DH center, or an inclusive-history project (Enslaved.org-style)? The choice tilts the whole project's NPOV posture and beneficiary priorities.
5. **DAR/SAR engagement:** will an authorized data-sharing path open, and on what terms — and does any partner-supplied data create NPOV/selection bias the inclusion mandate then has to counterbalance?
6. **Provenance mechanism:** named-graphs+PROV-O vs RDF-star (M0 decision) — and does the chosen unit cleanly support *both* citation provenance *and* allegiance/role qualification?
7. **Funded-lane extraction:** if Claude-assisted extraction is metered (funded lane), what per-batch budget cap and human-review ratio keep cost bounded while preserving the independent-auditor and zero-false-merge guarantees?
8. **Representativeness target:** what coverage thresholds (and for which under-represented groups, where sources permit) should the success metrics commit to, so "accurate" doesn't quietly mean "accurate but skewed"?
